HAICI Framework

The Human-AI Chemistry Index quantifies how effectively humans and AI systems collaborate. Unlike adoption metrics that count logins or usage frequency, HAICI measures the quality of the working relationship between a person and an AI tool. The framework is grounded in 87+ empirical studies spanning cognitive science, organizational psychology, and human-computer interaction.

Ci = 100 × (P × T) / (400 + L² + S²)

Variables (each scored 0 to 20):

P (Performance): Does the AI deliver real, measurable results? Encompasses accuracy, reliability, and task completion quality.

T (Trust): Do users rely on AI output without second-guessing? Measures calibrated trust, not blind faith. Users should trust appropriately based on the AI's actual capabilities.

L (Cognitive Load): Does the tool free mental bandwidth or consume it? Higher values indicate greater burden. An AI that requires constant supervision, complex prompting, or frequent error correction scores high on cognitive load.

S (Stress): Does AI feel like a partner or a burden? Higher values indicate greater friction. Measures anxiety, frustration, and the emotional cost of working with the tool.

Certification Bands:

Catalytic (75 to 100): The AI-human pairing accelerates work beyond what either could achieve alone. Rare and worth protecting.

Stable (60 to 74): Solid working relationship. The tool adds clear value with manageable overhead.

Provisional (45 to 59): The partnership works but has friction. Worth investigating what drags it down.

Unfit (0 to 44): The tool costs more than it delivers. Retrain, replace, or redesign the integration.

ROI Capture: min(Ci / 80, 1) × 100%. A chemistry score below 80 means you are leaving value on the table. Catalytic pairings capture near-full ROI. Unfit pairings waste investment.

Bilateral Extension: HAICI can be applied in both directions. How well does the human work with AI? And how well does the AI system accommodate different human working styles? This bilateral view reveals whether failures stem from tool design or user readiness.

Client Delivery Model: Assess (data collection, surveys, observation), Diagnose (score calculation, pattern analysis, root cause identification), Recommend (targeted interventions, training plans, tool configuration changes). Typical engagement runs 2 to 3 weeks per team.

Chemistry Law

Core Principle: Technology sets the potential. Culture determines the realization. A brilliant AI tool deployed into a resistant or unprepared culture will underperform a mediocre tool embraced by a ready one. This is the Chemistry Law, and it applies to every AI investment.

ROI Capture = min(Ci / 80, 1) × 100%

The Proof Scenarios: Consider two cases. A fantastic tool (P=18, T=16) deployed to L1-level users who struggle with basic cognitive load (L=2, S=3) yields a Chemistry Score near 69 and captures roughly 86% of potential ROI. Now take a complex tool (P=12, T=8) given to L5-level innovators dealing with significant friction (L=14, S=12) and the score drops to around 15, capturing barely 19% of ROI. The tool quality matters less than the cultural and cognitive fit.

The Ferrari Analogy: Buying a Ferrari does not make you a racing driver. Buying the best AI tools does not make your workforce AI-capable. Without the right skills, trust, and workflow integration, premium tools deliver economy-car results.

Governance Implication: Organizations should measure and optimize Chemistry Scores as rigorously as they track license costs and feature adoption. A procurement decision without a HAICI assessment is incomplete. A training program without pre-and-post HAICI measurement is flying blind.

L1 to L5 Consultant Proficiency Maturity Framework

Five levels of AI proficiency for consulting professionals. Each level represents a qualitative shift in how a consultant uses AI, not just frequency of use but depth of integration into their work.

L1

Compliant Operator

Follows instructions. Uses AI tools when told to, in prescribed ways. Can execute pre-built prompts and workflows. Does not adapt or improvise. Needs step-by-step guidance for any new AI task. The baseline. Everyone starts here.

L2

Efficient User

Uses AI tools independently for routine tasks. Understands which tool fits which task. Can modify prompts to improve output. Saves measurable time on repetitive work. Still treats AI as a tool, not a partner.

L3

Workflow Architect

Designs multi-step AI workflows for recurring consulting tasks. Chains tools together. Builds templates others can use. Thinks in systems, not single interactions. The pivot point between using AI and leveraging AI.

L4

Builder

Creates custom agents, automations, and integrations. Builds solutions that scale beyond personal use. Understands API patterns, data flows, and system design at a working level. Contributes to the organization's AI capability.

L5

Innovator

Defines new use cases. Sees opportunities others miss. Combines AI with domain expertise to create novel approaches. Teaches and mentors others. Shapes the organization's AI strategy. Not just using the tools but advancing how the organization thinks about AI.
